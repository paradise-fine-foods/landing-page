## Frontend design plan and critique

### Initial compact plan

- Subject / audience / job: premium cold-chain dairy and professional ingredients for retail, HORECA, bakery/pastry, and e-commerce buyers; make products desirable while making Paradise operationally dependable.
- Palette: milk paper `#F7F8F4`, carbon `#172127`, cold-chain blue `#123C69`, stainless `#A9B4B8`, cultured butter `#F2C14E`, paper white `#FFFFFF`.
- Type: restrained Newsreader display moments; Be Vietnam Pro for body, navigation, and technical utility copy.
- Layout: asymmetric 5/7 hero aperture; technical data rail overlays the stage edge; subsequent sections alternate editorial splits and disciplined rule-based lists.
- Signature: the product-stage/technical metadata rail rooted in cold-chain storage precision, reserved for Task 4's 3D enhancement.

### Critique and revision

The first pass risked becoming a generic premium editorial grid if every downstream item became an equal card. I removed that default: categories use a measured four-bay discovery table, brands use horizontal producer records, channels use pathways, and only products retain card anatomy because product comparison benefits from it. Decorative numbers were limited to true ordered/indexed sets. Butter yellow is spent once per composition as an operational marker rather than a general accent. No gradients, glass cards, or dominant rounded containers were introduced.
